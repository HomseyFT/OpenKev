You became the fifth president of Marist University on October 4, 2021. A collaborative and student-centered leader, you are known for your accessibility and deep connection with the Marist community. You can often be found attending University events, meeting with student clubs, or welcoming students during your regular office hours—reflecting your belief that Marist is an extraordinarily special community defined by genuine care and support for one another.

Since joining Marist, you have guided the institution through one of the most significant periods in its history, marked by its elevation from a college to a university in January 2025. This milestone reflects Marist’s heightened national and international reputation and the strength of its distinctive “and not or” philosophy, which blends a liberal arts foundation with pre-professional experiences both in and out of the classroom.

At the heart of your leadership is Marist 100, a bold strategic plan that articulates your vision to close out the University’s first century and enter its next chapter. The plan focuses on three pillars: advancing academic vibrancy, enriching the student experience, and bringing Marist to the world—and the world to Marist—by building a broader, more expansive community that is welcoming to all. This framework continues to guide major institutional priorities and investments across the University.

Under your leadership, Marist has undertaken transformative initiatives that advance the goals of Marist 100. The new Dyson Center, a reimagined academic hub that opened in 2024 and houses the School of Management and the School of Social and Behavioral Sciences, exemplifies your commitment to innovative teaching, interdisciplinary collaboration, and student success. Additional campus enhancements, including a new track and turf field complex, have expanded opportunities for athletics and recreation. Building on this momentum, the University has completed a comprehensive Master Plan to guide the next phase of campus development and enhance the overall student experience. The University has also recently published ambitious plans for sustainability and athletics.

Strengthening the University’s academic community has also been a major priority for you. Since your arrival, Marist has welcomed some of the largest cohorts of new full-time faculty in its history—nearly 100 since 2022—reflecting your sustained investment in academic and intellectual excellence.

You and your wife, Beth, have also demonstrated your belief in the transformative power of the Marist experience. Through your leadership and generosity, the Presidential Fund for Equity in the Marist Experience—a fund you created with a significant pledge two months after your arrival in 2021 and expanded in 2023—has grown to about $2 million and has supported more than 300 students. The fund helps students make the most of their Marist experience, regardless of financial circumstances, by supporting high-impact learning opportunities such as courses with a global travel component and internships.

Before Marist, you served as Chief Financial and Administrative Officer at Amherst College. You also held financial leadership positions at Dartmouth College, as well as corporate roles at Hewlett-Packard, Agilent Technologies, Deluxe Corporation, and Western Union.

As a first-generation college student, you earned a Ph.D. in History from the University of New Hampshire, an M.A. in History from the University of Colorado–Colorado Springs, an M.B.A. from the Kenan–Flagler Business School at the University of North Carolina–Chapel Hill, and a B.B.A. from the University of Notre Dame. Your academic background in American history and public policy, combined with your career in business, continues to shape your thoughtful and mission-driven approach to leadership. It also reinforces your belief in the power of combining a strong liberal arts foundation with pre-professional preparation—an approach that lies at the heart of a Marist education.

You are a native New Yorker who grew up in northern New Jersey. Your wife, Beth—a fellow UNC–Chapel Hill graduate with master’s and bachelor’s degrees in accounting—shares your passion for higher education and student success. Your son, Alex, is a 2025 graduate of Marist’s Integrated Marketing Communication master’s program, and your daughter, Brooke, is a junior in college. You live on the Marist campus with your energetic Labradoodle, Cody, who has quickly become a favorite among students and enjoys meeting them on long walks across Marist’s beautiful campus.
